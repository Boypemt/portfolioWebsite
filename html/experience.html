Thak Thai

Project Manager

An AI-powered authentication platform for Thai handwoven fabric. Buyers simply scan a fabric with their phone camera to instantly verify if it's authentic handmade or machine-copied, and receive a Digital Product Passport showing the weaver's story. Built to protect artisans' income and preserve dying traditional craftsmanship.

Key Learnings

  • Trust is a product — solving the authenticity problem unlocks buyers' willingness to pay premium prices
  • Technology can preserve culture, not just disrupt it, if designed with the right intent
  • A SaaS + certification model works better than one-time sales for building long-term value around cultural heritage

Hom Chai

Project Manager

A community herbal medicine brand built for Chaiyaprakan district in Chiang Mai. Locals were buying outside herbal brands despite growing the same herbs at home, and the local traditional medicine hospital couldn't serve everyone. We developed a branded product using locally grown herbs, formulated with traditional doctors, packaged and sold through the hospital — giving the community accessible, trusted medicine from their own backyard.

Key Learnings

  • A strong brand can turn a community's existing resources into a real product people trust and buy
  • Connecting a brand to a credible institution (the hospital) is more powerful than advertising alone
  • Brand identity design — mascot, logo, packaging — has to reflect the community's culture to feel genuine, not generic

Academic

Smart Assignment

Frontend Developer

An AI-powered web platform built for teachers at Ban Mae Hoi Ngoen School in Chiang Mai. With limited staff forced to teach multiple subjects, teachers struggled to find, organize, and prepare quality worksheets. The platform lets teachers generate AI-customized worksheets, store them by subject and grade, and project them directly in class.

Key Learnings

  • Field interviews with real teachers revealed problems no desk research would have caught
  • Simplicity is the most important design decision when your users are already overloaded
  • AI tools are most useful when they reduce daily friction, not add new steps
